Waterfall

follows a linear, sequential approach. It involves completing each phase of a project before moving on to the next, with little room for flexibility or iteration.

Agile

emphasizes collaboration, adaptive planning, and iterative development. It encourages cross-functional teams to deliver incremental value, respond to changes, and prioritize customer satisfaction.

Scrum

Focuses on iterative and incremental development. It emphasizes self-organizing teams and regular collaboration, with work divided into time-boxed sprints to deliver functional increments of a product.

Kanban

Uses visual boards to track and manage work. It promotes a continuous flow of tasks by limiting work in progress and emphasizing the importance of completing one task before starting another.
